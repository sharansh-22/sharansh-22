## Research Philosophy

<table>
<tr>
<td width="50%" valign="top">

#### Approach

Every project I undertake follows a deliberate research methodology:

1. **Define the question** — not the tool
2. **Design the experiment** — control variables, define baselines
3. **Collect evidence** — let data dictate direction
4. **Validate rigorously** — out-of-time splits, leakage audits, stress tests
5. **Report honestly** — negative results are findings, not failures

</td>
<td width="50%" valign="top">

#### Principles

- Negative experimental results eliminate incorrect assumptions and are documented with the same rigour as positive results
- Leakage-free evaluation is non-negotiable
- A model that explains *why* outweighs a model that predicts *more*
- Research provenance — every claim should be traceable to evidence
- Reproducibility over performance theatre

</td>
</tr>
</table>

### 🧪 [CRIS — Cascade Risk Intelligence System](https://github.com/sharansh-22/CRIS)
**Quantitative Research Program · Environmental Intelligence in Financial Decision Systems**

</div>

<br>

> A structured research program investigating whether macroeconomic and market signals (environmental intelligence) improve financial decision systems beyond borrower-centric models.

<table>
<tr>
<td width="50%" valign="top">

#### Research Design

- Controlled comparison: borrower-only vs. macro-augmented models
- Systematic correlation analysis of macroeconomic features
- Cascade architecture for staged signal integration
- Full documentation of methodology and experimental controls

</td>
<td width="50%" valign="top">

#### Key Findings

- **Borrower-centric models outperformed** direct macro integration
- Macroeconomic signals exhibited strong correlation but **limited incremental predictive value** in this experimental setting
- Negative results were **documented and published** — not discarded
- Clear future work directions defined from evidence

</td>
</tr>
